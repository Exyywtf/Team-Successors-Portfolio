Project Scope Statement

Initially, to give the team direction, we framed our project scope statement as a contract. It defines the project description, objectives, deliverables, and constraints while making role ownership explicit from the start. This gave every department a clear reference point when reviewing progress and helped keep decisions aligned across the team, with deliverables only considered complete once they had been signed off by the Heads of Department and our Mentor.

Resource Management & Budget

We created a cash flow forecast to estimate engineering, enterprise, and team management outflows, while predicting when those costs would occur if sponsor funding was delayed. Following the prudence accounting concept kept the forecast realistic and prevented outflows from being understated while we approached sponsors. We also maintained a shared Microsoft Excel expense report logging each purchase, its value, date, category, and invoice or receipt for reimbursement.

Communication

We developed a communication framework to keep internal coordination and external stakeholder updates clear throughout the competition.

Internal Communication External Communication
Internally, we used a structured communication system to keep every team member closely aligned on objectives, timelines, and individual responsibilities. Weekly meetings were used to review progress, set short-term targets, strengthen accountability through task management tools, and raise blockers early before they affected wider progress across the whole team. Externally, we built direct communication channels with stakeholders while removing unnecessary layers. A website accessible to sponsors and mentors provided updated financial information, clear goals, blockers, action items, and an outline of the team's timeline, making progress and support requirements easier to communicate clearly and consistently.
Roles & Responsibilities

Clear role allocation was essential to building a competitive STEM Racing team, so we used a RACI matrix to define each member's contribution across key deliverables. It strengthened accountability, ensured each deliverable was covered, and made responsibilities easy to assign and review.

Quality Control

Our quality control system used separate departmental chains of command to maintain the standard of every output while allowing work to progress without unrelated interference. Each deliverable was reviewed within the department best placed to assess its quality before moving upward for approval, creating a clear route for feedback and revision. This kept accountability visible, reduced unnecessary overlap, and helped us resolve issues early rather than later in the project.

Marketing Strategy

To maximise exposure, we adopted a three-step marketing strategy:

Step 1
Situational Analysis (SWOT)
Strengths
  • Highly capable team members with a clear commitment to excellence.
  • A distinct team identity shaped by our pace, position, and direction.
  • Strong mentorship and the foundation of a national team that placed 6th.
Weaknesses
  • A heavy academic workload during May and June, overlapping with competition dates.
  • Limited direct return for those less involved, such as students and parents.
  • No dedicated GFS facilities for car testing.
Opportunities
  • Access to 6,000+ students and 500+ staff at GFS.
  • Access to 100k+ LinkedIn followers and 20k+ Instagram followers.
  • Access to our sponsors' resources, expertise, and wider professional networks.
Threats
  • A highly competitive STEM competition, with 80+ teams in the most recent national finals.
